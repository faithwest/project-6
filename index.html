Bacon is a by-product of pigs. The word bacon means “meat from the back of an animal.” American bacon is made from the pork belly, which accounts for its trademark streaks of fat. European and Canadian bacon are still made from the back meat of a pig, which is considerably leaner. 70% of all bacon in the US is eaten at breakfast time. More than 2 billion pounds of bacon is produced each year in the US. The phrase “bringing home the bacon” originated back in the 12th century when a church in England offered a side of bacon to any man who could swear before the church that he had not fought or quarreled with his wife for a year and a day. Any man who could “bring home the bacon” was highly respected in his community.
